About

I am a PhD student in Civil Engineering at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln. My research focuses on transportation engineering, with a special interest in autonomous vehicle (AV) safety, pedestrian–vehicle interactions, and the use of machine learning to improve predictive models in transportation systems.

I am passionate about integrating advanced data sources and innovative methods to enhance road safety and transportation equity.

Research

My research explores autonomous vehicle (AV) interactions with pedestrians, headway stability in AV platoons, and the integration of novel data sources into crash prediction models. This includes analyzing post-encroachment time (PET), time-to-collision (TTC), and other safety indicators using the Third Generation Simulation (TGSIM) datasets.

I also examine the role of smart data sources like StreetLight calibrated traffic volumes and Mapillary open-street-view detections in pedestrian crash analysis. My work highlights the trade-offs between traditional generalized linear models (GLMs) and modern machine learning (ML) approaches in safety studies.
